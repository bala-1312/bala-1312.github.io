The Technique2025 · 11

Every idea you’ve had most probably is an amalgamation of content you’ve absorbed through your senses. So, there is always going to be some quantity of that reflecting in the work that we do.

Just like we inherit traits from parents, we inherit artistic instincts from past influences.

A nihilistic person could have consumed existential matter through reading, video essays, movies or maybe they just don't see the point of life. Perhaps it's just physics and chemistry staged a coup which resulted in a conscious being. To them.
An optimist is more jovial, maybe, just because he's also a nihilist, in that the universe does not dream of you, so why bother with the meaninglessness instead of embracing the absurdity.

Every moment is a choice.
Every choice that we make, it could be the selection of a font style, a course, a book, a sigh, a reply are linked by past experience to some degree. This goes on in our heads, and when the time comes to produce something, our brain gives it on a platter (just that it's transformed now) if you haven't noticed, try it next time.

So if we are so vulnerable to implementing the things that we experienced, there should be a guilt-free way to do it, right?

Copying is as boring as things can get, but introduce a bit of deviation and see the way things now take shape. That is fun.

Like the color white in a selector, it's a mix of RGB (255, 255, 255), any deviation results in a different tint. Hello Color blinds!

So, instead of blatantly copying the content that you found amusing into your work, rather identify the technique the creator used, find what you liked or were intrigued by?

More often than not, it's the how instead of the what, that we find amusing.
